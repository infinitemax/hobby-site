Limestone

Lots of holds, no friction

Cliffs and quarries

Limestone climbing areas tend to be found in the south of the Peak District. Generally these are sport climbing routes, i.e. there are bolts pre-drilled into the rock. Many abandonned quarries have been turned into excellent spots for climbing.

Holds aplenty

Climbers are drawn to limestone crags for their distinct features, which include pockets, cracks, and intricate formations. These natural holds and features make for engaging and technical climbs, requiring precise footwork and creative problem-solving.

Strong fingers...

Unfortunately, although there are lots of holds, they are often very small, requiring paritcularly strong fingers! This, and the fact that many routes are very long, makes climbing limestone a strenuous endeavour.

Underwater

Limestone is a sedimentary rock primarily composed of calcium carbonate deposited over millions of years from the accumulation of marine organisms' remains. Its formation often occurs in warm, shallow seas. So the Peak District was once underwater!
