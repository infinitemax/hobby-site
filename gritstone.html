Gritstone

Lots of friction, nothing to hold

Sandstone

Typically found in regions with ancient geological histories, such as the Peak District, gritstone (or just 'grit') is a type of very hard sandstone, primarily composed of grains of mineral particles like quartz and feldspar.

Friction

Gritstone's distinctive rough texture and excellent friction properties make it ideal for climbing, but also for making millstones - many of which can still be seen abandonned in the Peak District today!

Technical

Due to the nature of grit, it often has very slopey holds, with little to grab on to. This means that climbing on grit can be extremely delecate and technical, requiring strong hands and excellent balance. It can be terrifying, but for some reason we all love it!

Magic!

In spite of these difficulties, grit is revered as an almost magical rock to climb on. It's challenge is definitely part of the appeal. When a climber elsewhere in the world has finished a difficult route it's not uncommon to hear people in Sheffield say 'well yeah, but what have they done on grit??'
